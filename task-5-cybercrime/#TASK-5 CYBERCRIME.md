**#TASK-5 CYBERCRIME**



**Ransomware Case Study: Attack on a Small Healthcare Clinic**

**Ransomware is malicious software that encrypts victims' files or locks systems, demanding payment for decryption keys. It has surged in recent years, crippling organizations worldwide.**



**How It Happens (Step-by-Step)**

**Attackers typically follow a structured chain. First, they conduct reconnaissance to identify vulnerabilities, like outdated software. Initial compromise occurs via phishing emails with malicious attachments, as in a case where a clinic receptionist opened a fake vendor invoice. The malware then persists by establishing footholds, escalates privileges, and moves laterally across networks. It gathers and exfiltrates data before encrypting files, displaying a ransom note demanding cryptocurrency, often with threats to leak stolen data.**



**Usual Targets**

**Healthcare providers, like small clinics and hospitals, are prime targets due to sensitive patient data and urgent need for access—critical services can't afford downtime. Businesses in retail, finance, and critical infrastructure face high attack rates, with 65% of financial organizations hit by 2024. Individuals are also vulnerable via compromised apps or data breaches.**



**Consequences**

**In the clinic scenario, all records, schedules, and billing locked instantly, halting operations for days. Victims face financial losses (ransom plus recovery costs exceeding millions in some cases), data leaks risking patient privacy, and reputational damage. Broader impacts include service disruptions; for example, Ryuk ransomware caused over $60 million in global damages.**
